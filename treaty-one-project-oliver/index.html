Welcome to the website of Treaty 1

What is Treaty 1?

Treaty 1 was the first numbered treaty that was signed in Canada on August 3, 1871 as an agreement for them to share the land with First Nations people and give them special benefits. This laid the groundwork for all of the rest of the numbered treaties in Canada. This treaty was signed in the ″stone fort″ which is Lower Fort Garry in modern-day Manitoba. This is the treaty that I, the person making this website, resides on and also all the people living in Winnipeg.

What is a Treaty?

A treaty was a written agreement between two Nations that was approved and signed by both of their leaders. In this case, a numbered treaty in Canada would be an agreement between a group of First Nations people or communties and Canada over a part of land. In exchange, These First Nations people will have land reserved for them which is called ″reserve land″

What Makes this Treaty Unique from Others?

What makes this Treaty different from other Treaties in Canada is that it was the first Numbered Treaty signed in Canada. This became the foundation to all the other treaties being signed and helped Canada to learn and know how to agree with the First Nations people that were living on that land thousands of years before they came. They also established their sovereignty of certain parts of Canada that would have been taken over by other countries if those Treaties haven't been signed at all.
